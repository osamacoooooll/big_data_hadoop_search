18547927	O'Brien Peak	O'Brien Peak () is a rock peak, 670 m, standing 3 nautical miles (6 km) west of the north extremity of Medina Peaks, along the edge of the Ross Ice Shelf. Discovered in December 1929 by the Byrd Antarctic Expedition geological party under Laurence Gould, and named by Byrd for John S. O'Brien, surveyor with that party. ==See also== *Paradise Ridge ==References== Category:Mountains of the Ross Dependency Category:Amundsen Coast
