20265295	O'Brien, Argentina	Eduardo O'Brien is a town in Buenos Aires Province, Argentina, about west of Buenos Aires, which was officially founded on 21 March 1909 on land provided by Eduardo O'Brien (1836-1912), who was born in County Wexford, Ireland and emigrated to Brazil at the age of fourteen with his parents Patrick (1802-1892) and Frances (1802-1890).Irish Place Names and Landmarks in Argentina When in 1907 a railway was built across his estate, O'Brien donated land for the construction of a railway station. The station carried his name, later, in the year 1935, it was changed to General O'Brien. ==References== == External links == *Estacion O'Brien by Jorge Zanela (in Spanish) *City of O'Brien website Category:Populated places in Buenos Aires Province
